What is the 111 Contact Code?

In November 2020, the Commerce Commission finalised the 111 Contact Code, which ensures that vulnerable customers (or persons on their behalf) have reasonable access to an appropriate means of contacting the 111 emergency service in the event of a power failure.

This establishes support for customers that have moved to new home phone technologies like fibre and fixed wireless, which (unlike traditional landlines) require a power supply in the home to work. This means that they will not work in a power cut without an independent power source.

If you are still on a copper landline connection, this will not apply to you. However, if you move to a fibre or fixed wireless home phone solution in the future – and you are a vulnerable customer– you will need to keep this in mind.

What is happening to address this?

Voyager, along with other telecommunication service providers, is creating registers for our vulnerable customers. Customers that are registered on this list will be contacted by us to arrange an alternative solution for you to access 111 in emergencies. There will be no cost for this alternative.

We are currently working through the best options for these alternatives and will provide them to Vulnerable Customers by 1 August 2021, as per the requirements of the 111 Contact Code.

Voyager will contact all customers with information on how to apply for registration for our Vulnerable Customers register by 1 May 2021. In the meantime, if you believe you will fall under this category, please read further for more information and to find out how you can indicate this to us in advance.

Am I a Vulnerable Customer?

Under the 111 Contact Code, to qualify as a Vulnerable Customer you must fall under all of the following:

  • Have a voice over broadband calling solution with Voyager
  • Not have access to a traditional copper landline
  • Show that you (or someone in your household) are at particular risk of needing to contact the 111 emergency service due to health, safety or disability reasons
  • Not have a mobile (on any provider) that can be used as an alternative at home or an alternative power source
  • Not have another means for contacting the 111 emergency service at your premises that that can be operated for a continuous eight hour period in the event of a power failure

NOTE: The Code applies only to landline voice services. Broadband-only customers cannot apply for registration.

How to apply to our Vulnerable Customer register

While we continue to develop our application process, customers who believe they may fall under the Vulnerable Customer status can indicate this to us in advance by filling out the below form.

As this form is purely an indication of those that believe they qualify, we ask that you do not send us any personal or detailed information beyond what is asked on the form. We will be in touch by 1 May 2021 with the official application instructions.

This form is NOT the official application; it is simply a way for Voyager to keep track of early indications of those who believe they qualify so that we can ensure you are contacted to apply, come 1 May 2021.

When it comes time for the official application, you (or someone on your behalf) will need to demonstrate that you do not have access to a mobile phone as an alternative to your landline, or an alternative power source. You will also need to provide sufficient evidence that you are particularly at risk of requiring 111.

Examples of documents that can be used as evidence:
  • A completed Electricity Authority ‘Notice of Potential Medically Dependent Customer (MDC) Status’ form, which includes a certification from a DHB, private hospital or GP.
  • A protection order
  • A letter from a health care provider
  • Documentation of impairment (such as an ID card)

Alternatively, you can provide details of a nominated person we can contact to verify that you, or the person in your household, are at risk of requiring 111 emergency services. This nominated person must be someone who, by virtue of their occupation, is competent to give an opinion on whether you (or the person you are applying on behalf of) is at particular risk of requiring the 111 emergency service.

Examples:
  • Health practitioner
  • Police officer
  • Registered social worker
  • Lawyer
  • Family court judge

This page will be updated with more information as we finalise our application process.

What can I use as an alternative means of contacting 111 emergency services in the event of a power failure?

There are several options for alternative means of contacting 111 emergency services if a power outage should occur. Voyager suggests that a fully charged mobile phone should be kept close by. An uninterruptible power supply (UPS) can also be installed as a way to keep your telecommunications services powered and running.

Customers on our Vulnerable Customer register that don't have a mobile phone or alternative means of contacting 111 (such as battery back-up or solar power) will be provided an alternative solution appropriate for their individual circumstances.

Telecommunications devices that may not work in a power outage

During a power outage, any landline that requires power will not work unless you have a backup power source. This includes:

  • Landlines using Fibre, Wireless Broadband, and Copper technologies where power is required, including Voyager Voice residential and business VoIP plans
  • Cordless phones
  • House alarms
